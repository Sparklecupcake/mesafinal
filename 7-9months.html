7-9 Months Milestone/Checklist:

If your baby is 7-9 months, here is a general checklist on what their progress should look like! At this age, consulting a doctor is recommended if your baby has 7 or fewer checks. Additionally, your baby is due for a checkup! According to the AAP, the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that your baby is due for general developmental screening. Please check with your pediatrician or doctor regarding this. For more resources, check this link: https://www.cdc.gov/ncbddd/actearly/hcp/index.html?CDC_AA_refVal=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cdc.gov%2Factearly%2Fhealthcare%2Findex.html If there is no doctor available, look at this link which briefly explains how you can contact your local healthcare providers and set up a proper healthcare treatment for your baby: https://www.healthcare.gov/what-if-im-pregnant-or-plan-to-get-pregnant/


What MOST babies do by this age:


Language/Communication Milestones:

-When they want to be picked up, they lift their arms

-Makes other sounds like 'babababa' or 'mamamama'


Movement/Physical Development Milestones:

-Can move things from one hand to the other hand

-To get food, they use their fingers to 'rake' it towards themselves

-Can sit without assistance (by themself)
